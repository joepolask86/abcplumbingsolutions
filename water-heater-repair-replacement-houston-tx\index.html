Signs You Need Help Now

Recognizing the signs of a malfunctioning water heater can save you from costly damages and discomfort. If you notice inconsistent water temperatures, strange noises coming from the heater, or water pooling around the unit, it’s time to act. Ignoring these symptoms can lead to significant issues such as leaks, water damage, or even complete heater failure.

Delaying repairs can also increase repair costs and disrupt your home life. Hot water is essential for bathing, cleaning, and cooking. When problems arise, swift action is critical to restore your comfort and prevent further damage. Don't let a minor issue escalate into a major emergency.

FAQs

What are common signs of a failing water heater?

Common signs include inconsistent water temperature, strange noises, and leaks around the unit.

How long does a water heater typically last?

On average, a traditional water heater lasts 10-15 years, while tankless models can last up to 20 years with proper maintenance.

Can I install a water heater myself?

While DIY installation is possible, it’s not recommended due to safety and compliance issues. Professional installation ensures adherence to local codes.

What should I do if I have a water leak?

Immediately turn off the water supply to the heater and contact a professional plumber to assess and repair the leak.

How often should I have my water heater serviced?

It’s advisable to have your water heater inspected and maintained at least once a year to ensure optimal performance.
